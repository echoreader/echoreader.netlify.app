## How to Make (or Buy) a Bubbly Sourdough Starter

Your *sourdough starter* is the heart of this *Jamie Oliver sourdough*. I made mine from scratch using King Arthur flour and filtered tap water no weird ingredients.

### Day-by-Day Starter Schedule (7 Days)

| Day | Feeding Ratio (Flour:Water) | What to Expect                     |
|-----|-----------------------------|------------------------------------|
| 1   | 1:1 (50g each)              | Smells like wet flour             |
| 3   | 1:1:1 (discard + 50g each)  | Bubbles! Smells like yogurt       |
| 5   | 1:2:2                       | Doubles in 4–6 hours              |
| 7   | Ready when it doubles fast  | Fruity, beery aroma               |

**Pro tip**: I name my starter (mine’s “Bubba”) and keep it on the counter in a Weck jar from Target. Feed it daily if room temp is above 70°F; fridge it weekly otherwise.

If you’re short on time, buy a mature starter from Breadtopia or local bakeries in cities like San Francisco or Brooklyn. Just make sure it’s 100% hydrated (equal flour and water by weight).

## Ingredients and Tools You’ll Need

I shop at Costco and local grocery chains nothing fancy required.

### Core Ingredients (1 Large Loaf)

- **450g bread flour** (I use King Arthur or Bob’s Red Mill)
- **50g whole wheat or rye flour** (adds depth)
- **350g filtered water** (75–80°F)
- **100g active sourdough starter**
- **10g fine sea salt**

No banneton? I proof in a floured tea towel inside a colander.

## Step-by-Step Sourdough Instructions (Jamie Oliver Style)

Here’s my adapted *sourdough bread recipe* based on Jamie’s no-knead, high-hydration method. Total hands-on time: **15 minutes**.

### Step 1: Autolyse (30 Minutes)
Mix flours and 325g water until shaggy. Rest. This hydrates the dough and kickstarts gluten.

### Step 2: Add Starter and Salt
Dissolve starter in remaining 25g water. Add to dough with salt. Pinch and fold for 2 minutes until incorporated. It’ll be sticky *that’s good*.

### Step 3: Bulk Fermentation (8–12 Hours)
Cover and let sit at room temp (68–75°F ideal). Every 30–60 minutes for the first 3 hours, do **stretch and folds**:
1. Wet hands
2. Grab one side, stretch up, fold over center
3. Rotate bowl 90°, repeat 4x

After 8 hours, my dough usually triples with big bubbles.

### Step 4: Shape the Loaf
Turn onto lightly floured counter. Letter-fold, then roll into a tight ball. Proof seam-side up in floured basket for 2–3 hours (or fridge overnight for better flavor).

### Step 5: Bake Like a Pro
Preheat Dutch oven at 450°F for 45 minutes.  
Score loaf with a lame or sharp knife.  
Bake covered 25 minutes, uncovered 20–25 until deep golden.  
**Internal temp should hit 205–210°F**.

Cool on rack **at least 2 hours** I know, torture.

## Troubleshooting Your Sourdough (I’ve Messed Up Plenty)

| Problem                  | Cause                              | Fix                                      |
|--------------------------|------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Dense, gummy crumb       | Underproofed or weak starter       | Let bulk ferment longer; feed starter    |
| Flat loaf                | Overproofed or poor shaping        | Reduce proof time; tighten boule         |
| Pale crust               | No steam or low oven temp          | Preheat Dutch oven fully; use ice cube   |
| Too sour                 | Long cold proof                    | Bake same day or reduce rye in starter   |

## FAQ

**Can I make Jamie Oliver sourdough without a Dutch oven?**  
Yes! Use a heavy baking sheet with a metal bowl over the loaf + ice cubes in a pan below for steam.

**How long does the sourdough starter last?**  
Forever, if fed. I’ve revived 3-month-neglected starter with 2–3 daily feedings.

**Why is my dough too sticky to handle?**  
High hydration (78%) is normal. Use wet hands and minimal flour resist adding more.

**Can I freeze the dough?**  
Shape, proof 75%, then freeze. Thaw in fridge overnight and finish proofing before baking.

**What’s the best flour for beginners?**  
King Arthur Bread Flour it’s consistent and widely available at Walmart, Costco, or online.
