## FAQ

  <summary>How can these desserts be gluten-free without using special flours?</summary>
  <p>The simplicity comes from relying on naturally gluten-free bases like nuts (almond or peanut butter), eggs, coconut products (flour, shreds), and fruits. This avoids the need for complex gluten-free flour blends, keeping the ingredient list short.</p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>What is the most common ingredient used in these simple recipes?</summary>
  <p><strong>Eggs and nut butter</strong> (like peanut or almond butter) are staple ingredients. Eggs provide structure and binding without gluten, while nut butter serves as the base, fat, and protein source for many quick, no-bake, or three-ingredient recipes.</p>
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Are "few ingredients" recipes still considered healthy?</summary>
  <p>Generally, yes. Fewer ingredients mean less chance for processed fillers, artificial flavors, or excess sugars. By focusing on whole, recognizable foods (like fruit, nuts, and healthy fats), the recipes are typically more nutrient-dense and naturally better for you.</p>
</details>

</details>
